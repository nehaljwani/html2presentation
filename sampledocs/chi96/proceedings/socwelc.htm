From the ACM/SIGCHI Chair and Executive Vice-Chair

On behalf of the Executive Committee of ACM's Special Interest Group on Computer-Human Interaction (ACM/SIGCHI), welcome to CHI 96, SIGCHI'S annual conference on Human-Computer Interaction. Each CHI conference offers a snapshot of the HCI field as it grows and evolves, of the people who define the HCI field, and of the people who collectively form SIGCHI. These proceedings provide a history of the growth and evolution of HCI. As HCI has evolved, so have these proceedings evolved As an experiment in electronic publishing, the CHI 96 Proceedings are available over the world wide web. We are grateful to the CHI 96 committee for taking this step and look forward to using what they learn this year in evolving proceedings in future years.

We also welcome you to participate in SIGCHI, a global society of over 5,000 people with a common interest in HCI. While the CHI conference is the most visible activity of SIGCHI, we also:

  • support conferences in computer-supported cooperative work (CSCW), multimedia (MM), user interface software and technology (UIST), user interface software and technology (UIST), design of interactive systems (DIS), and intelligent user interfaces (IWIUI).
  • distribute the highly-respected quarterly SIGCHI Bulletin to members and others
  • supported the "birth" and continue to support the evolution of two new publications - Transactions on Computer-Human Interactions (TOCHI), a member of the ACM Transactions series, and interactions, ACM's first magazine devoted to HCI practitioners.
  • provide support to a number of activities proposed by our members that provide benefit both to SIGCHI and the field of HCI. The SIGCHI Development Fund supports many of these activities and Calls for Proposals are distributed at this conference and appear frequently in the SIGCHI Bulletin.
SIGCHI is a vibrant and growing community and we invite your continued participation. This conference is one of the forums that SIGCHI provides for people to meet, both formally and informally, to exchange views, to share experiences, and to learn. These forums are not just attended by SIGCHI members, they are defined by SIGCHI members. We invite you to join and stay involved in this community and to define the forums of the future. We also invite you to join with other volunteers in providing SIGCHI's leadership.

Finally, on behalf of ACM/SIGCHI, we thank and congratulate the conference committee and all the volunteers who make this conference possible and who ensure a valuable experience for all of us. Thanks for a job well done!

Welcome to CHI 96 and to SIGCHI

Mike Atwood
ACM/SIGCHI Chair

Guy Boy
